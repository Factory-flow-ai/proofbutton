# ProofButton Kickstarter Campaign Video Script
## 90-Second Campaign Video

---

## TIMING & STRUCTURE

| Section | Time | Duration |
|---------|------|----------|
| Hook | 0-5s | 5 seconds |
| Problem Montage | 5-20s | 15 seconds |
| The Solution | 20-45s | 25 seconds |
| How It Works | 45-55s | 10 seconds |
| Credibility | 55-70s | 15 seconds |
| Call to Action | 70-80s | 10 seconds |
| Logo/Tagline | 80-90s | 10 seconds |

---

## SCENE-BY-SCENE BREAKDOWN

### SECTION 1: THE HOOK (0-5 seconds)

**Scene 1A: Medicine Cabinet | Interior, Warm Morning Light**

- **Visual**: Close-up of a teenage boy (Linkin, ~14) in bed. Mother enters, silhouetted against doorway light.
- **Camera**: Medium shot, eye-level, slightly angled down toward boy
- **Lighting**: Soft morning sun through bedroom window; warm color temperature
- **Action**: Mother gestures, speaking. Boy rolls eyes dramatically, pulling blanket up.
- **VoiceOver** (Mom's voice, warm but exasperated): "Did you take your medicine?"
- **On-Screen Text**: None
- **Sound**: Gentle alarm clock ticking in background
- **Music**: Soft, slightly comedic underscoring begins (light piano)
- **Timing**: 0-5 seconds

**Key Notes**:
- Establish the problem with humor and relatability
- Mother's tone conveys she genuinely doesn't know the answer
- Boy's eye-roll is the audience's reaction—we've all been here

---

### SECTION 2: PROBLEM MONTAGE (5-20 seconds)

This section uses rapid cuts (1-2 seconds each) to show multiple household chaos scenarios. Visual style: handheld, naturalistic, slightly comedic but grounded in reality.

---

**Scene 2A: Chicken Coop Chaos | Exterior, Afternoon Light**

- **Visual**: Parent (dad/mom) standing near kitchen/living room, hands on hips, yelling toward upstairs
- **Camera**: Wide shot, natural lighting, slight handheld shake for authenticity
- **Lighting**: Bright, natural afternoon light from windows
- **Action**: Parent shouts frustratedly. Quick cut to boy outside near chicken coop, doing something unrelated (kicking soccer ball, checking phone)
- **VoiceOver** (Parent, frustrated, yelling off-screen): "Did you feed the chickens?!"
- **Boy Response** (defensive, yelling back, off-screen): "YES!" (but we see he clearly hasn't yet)
- **On-Screen Text**: None (let the action speak)
- **Sound**: Natural ambience (wind, distant chicken clucks), echo of yelling voices
- **Music**: Builds slightly in tempo and intensity
- **Timing**: 5-7 seconds (2-second cut)

**Key Notes**:
- Humor from the disconnect between answer and reality
- Show the parent's skepticism—they don't believe the kid

---

**Scene 2B: Bathroom Desperation | Interior, Harsh Fluorescent Light**

- **Visual**: Person (could be teen or adult) sitting on toilet, frantically yelling, door closed
- **Camera**: Wide shot from bathroom doorway perspective, or slightly comedic angle
- **Lighting**: Bathroom overhead lights; slightly harsh/clinical to contrast with warm family scenes
- **Action**: Person yells urgently, hand up as if signaling. Cuts to hallway outside—no one is near; everyone is in other rooms.
- **VoiceOver** (Person, desperate, slightly panicked): "I NEED TOILET PAPER!"
- **Audio Response**: Silence. A faint TV sound from elsewhere. No one heard.
- **On-Screen Text**: None
- **Sound**: Bathroom echo, frustrated sigh, background household noise (TV, distant voices)
- **Music**: Comedic sting; tempo picks up
- **Timing**: 7-10 seconds (3-second cut)

**Key Notes**:
- Most relatable household pain point
- Use isolation of the bathroom to reinforce that no one hears
- Physical comedy without being crude

---

**Scene 2C: Dinner Call Ignored | Interior, Kitchen & Stairwell**

- **Visual**: Mother in kitchen (warm lighting, dinner cooking on stove, smell implied), calling up the stairs. Cut to upstairs—kids in rooms, doors closed, ignoring the call.
- **Camera**: Kitchen shot (medium, warm), then cut to upstairs hallway (wider, showing closed doors)
- **Lighting**: Warm kitchen light contrasts with cooler upstairs hallway lighting
- **Action**: Mom calls out enthusiastically. Immediate cut to kids not moving—one on bed scrolling phone, one gaming
- **VoiceOver** (Mom, cheerful but unheard): "DINNER'S READY!"
- **Response**: Silence. No movement. No acknowledgment.
- **On-Screen Text**: None
- **Sound**: Dinner sounds (sizzling, steam), then muffled upstairs sounds (typing, controller clicks)
- **Music**: Tempo continues to build; slightly anxious undertone
- **Timing**: 10-12 seconds (2-second cut)

**Key Notes**:
- Universal family frustration
- Show the disconnect between what the parent wants and what's happening
- Warm kitchen contrasts with disconnected upstairs spaces

---

**Scene 2D: Lighting Struggle | Interior, Dark Hallway**

- **Visual**: Someone (adult or teen) in a dark hallway/room at night, fumbling along the wall for a light switch, squinting, frustrated
- **Camera**: Handheld, slightly shaky, from third-person perspective
- **Lighting**: Minimal (dark hallway, maybe moonlight or distant light source)
- **Action**: Person slides hand along wall, misses switch, continues fumbling awkwardly
- **VoiceOver** (Person, muttering frustration): "Where is the...?" (trails off)
- **Sound**: Hand sliding on wall, frustrated sigh
- **Music**: Brief stinger note—peak of frustration
- **Timing**: 12-15 seconds (1-second cut, but feels longer due to the struggle)

**Key Notes**:
- Quick transition to the problem being broader than just family communication
- Light-hearted frustration
- Visual representation of friction in daily life

---

**Music & Sound Design for Montage**:
- Tempo gradually increases from 0-20 seconds
- Each scene gets a small comedic sting or sound effect
- Underlying bass line builds tension
- By second 20, the music reaches a crescendo—ready for release

---

### SECTION 3: THE SOLUTION (20-45 seconds)

**Tone Shift**: Same family, same situations, but now with ProofButton. The music shifts to uplifting, satisfying, triumphant. Every scene has a resolution.

---

**Scene 3A: Medicine Confirmed | Interior, Bedroom (Same as 1A)**

- **Visual**: Same bedroom, same morning light. Linkin sits up, reaches to nightstand. Button visible (labeled "MEDICINE" with custom text). **Close-up of finger pressing button.**
- **Camera**: Medium shot, then zoom/cut to extreme close-up of button press
- **Lighting**: Warm morning light, button catches light with satisfying gleam
- **Action**: **PRESS** — button clicks satisfyingly. Immediate cut to: Mom's phone buzzing, SMS text message appears on lock screen: "Medicine taken ✓ 8:45 AM"
- **VoiceOver** (Warm, satisfied narrator or Mom's voice): "His phone buzzes with proof."
- **On-Screen Text** (on phone screen): "Medicine taken ✓ 8:45 AM"
- **Sound**: Satisfying mechanical click, SMS notification sound (subtle, cheerful)
- **Music**: Uplifting chord; energy shifts positive
- **Timing**: 20-25 seconds (5-second beat)

**Key Notes**:
- First solution moment—show the text message arrival (buzzing phone is the hero shot)
- Phone is displaying a real text message, not an app notification
- This works on ANY phone—flip phone, smartphone, old phone
- Music shift signals we're in the solution space now

---

**Scene 3B: Chickens Fed, Confirmed | Exterior, Chicken Coop**

- **Visual**: Same boy as before, now at chicken coop door. ProofButton mounted on fence post (custom "CHICKENS" label). **Close-up of button press.**
- **Camera**: Medium shot, then zoom to button
- **Lighting**: Bright afternoon sun; golden hour warmth
- **Action**: **PRESS** — satisfying click. Immediate cut to: Dad's phone buzzing, SMS text appears: "Chickens fed ✓ 3:22 PM". Dad reads text, nods, satisfied.
- **VoiceOver** (Warm narrator): "Dad gets a text."
- **On-Screen Text** (on phone screen): "Chickens fed ✓ 3:22 PM"
- **Sound**: Satisfying click, chicken clucks (now happy, content), SMS notification
- **Music**: Uplifting, reinforces positive tone
- **Timing**: 25-30 seconds (5-second beat)

**Key Notes**:
- Show button can be anywhere outdoors (mounted on fence)
- Text message is the proof—simple, reliable, works everywhere
- Dad's satisfied nod = "I believe my kid did the chore because I have proof"

---

**Scene 3C: Bathroom SOS Solved | Interior, Bathroom**

- **Visual**: Same person in bathroom, now reaching for a ProofButton mounted on wall near toilet. Button labeled "TOILET PAPER" with appropriate humor/icon. **Close-up of button press.**
- **Camera**: Medium shot, then zoom to button detail
- **Lighting**: Bathroom light, but now warmer tone (color grading shift)
- **Action**: **PRESS** — satisfying click. Cut to: Family member in another room gets SMS text: "Toilet paper needed! 2:15 PM". They immediately get up and grab supplies, heading to bathroom.
- **VoiceOver** (Warm narrator): "Help arrives instantly."
- **On-Screen Text** (on family member's phone): "Toilet paper needed! 2:15 PM"
- **Sound**: Button click, phone SMS notification (cheerful)
- **Music**: Triumphant, satisfying resolution
- **Timing**: 30-35 seconds (5-second beat)

**Key Notes**:
- Text message is instant communication (no app needed for receiver)
- Show physical response (family member getting up) to prove efficacy
- Humor is maintained but outcome is genuinely useful
- Works even if family is in different rooms—simple text, instant action

---

**Scene 3D: Dinner Announcement | Interior, Kitchen & Upstairs**

- **Visual**: Mom in kitchen (warm, inviting lighting) with ProofButton labeled "DINNER" on the counter nearby. She **presses the button.** Cut to: Upstairs—multiple phones buzz with SMS texts. Kids look at phones, immediately get up and head downstairs.
- **Camera**: Kitchen wide shot, button close-up, then cut to upstairs (handheld, capturing quick responses)
- **Lighting**: Warm kitchen light; cooler upstairs light
- **Action**: Mom presses button—**satisfying click**. Upstairs, phones buzz with text: "Dinner is ready! 5:45 PM". Kids see text, put down phones/controllers, immediately respond.
- **VoiceOver** (Mom's voice, warm and clear): "One press. Everyone knows."
- **On-Screen Text** (on kids' phone screens): "Dinner is ready! 5:45 PM"
- **Sound**: Button click, SMS notification sounds, kids responding (doors opening, footsteps)
- **Music**: Triumphant, family-oriented theme
- **Timing**: 35-40 seconds (5-second beat)

**Key Notes**:
- Strongest family-bonding moment
- Show immediate response from kids (text notification creates action)
- Text message is the trigger—simple, reliable, works for everyone
- Music swells here—emotional peak

---

**Scene 3E: Goodnight Confirmation | Interior, Bedroom**

- **Visual**: Teenager in bed at night, reaching to bedside. ProofButton labeled "GOODNIGHT" on nightstand. **Button press close-up.** Parents' phone across the house buzzes with confirmation text.
- **Camera**: Wide shot of room, then extreme close-up of button, cut to parents' phone
- **Lighting**: Bright overhead transitioning to dim nightlight
- **Action**: **PRESS** — satisfying click. Parent's phone buzzes: "Goodnight ✓ 9:45 PM". Teen's face glows content. Parent smiles, satisfied (kid actually did bedtime).
- **VoiceOver** (Warm, gentle narrator): "Bedtime confirmed."
- **On-Screen Text** (on parent's phone): "Goodnight ✓ 9:45 PM"
- **Sound**: Button click, SMS notification, ambient night sounds (gentle)
- **Music**: Gentle, calming resolution; music softens
- **Timing**: 40-45 seconds (5-second beat)

**Key Notes**:
- Emotional payoff: proof that kid actually did bedtime routine
- SMS is the magic—parent gets confirmation from anywhere
- Teen's smile + parent's smile = mutual satisfaction
- Music begins to soften for transition to next section

---

**Music & Sound Design for Solution Section**:
- Dramatic shift from problem montage frustration to uplifting, satisfying resolution
- Each button press is rewarded with satisfying mechanical click + positive audio feedback
- Smart home integrations (Alexa, lights) feel seamless and delightful
- By second 45, music has reached an uplifting peak, ready for technical explanation

---

### SECTION 4: HOW IT WORKS (45-55 seconds)

**Tone**: Educational, impressive, simple. Use animation or clear visual explanation.

---

**Scene 4: Animation Explainer | Graphical/Animated Sequence**

- **Visual**: Clean, modern animated diagram. Show the energy flow from button press to action.
  - **Frame 1** (45-47s): Hand pressing button, kinetic energy visualization (glowing lines radiating outward from button)
  - **Frame 2** (47-48s): Energy transfers to internal mechanism, capacitor charges (animated circuit board, glowing components)
  - **Frame 3** (48-49s): Bluetooth signal radiates from button (BLE icon, radio waves)
  - **Frame 4** (49-50s): Signal reaches hub device (WiFi router, node graphic)
  - **Frame 5** (50-51s): Hub sends signal to cloud/home ecosystem (Amazon Alexa, Google Home, etc. logos light up)
  - **Frame 6** (51-52s): Smart home device executes action (light turns on, speaker plays sound, notification appears on phone)
  - **Frame 7** (52-55s): Final frame shows the complete cycle with text overlay

- **Camera**: Animated camera movement; zoom into button, pan through circuit, zoom out to whole home ecosystem
- **Lighting**: Clean, tech-forward. Blue/white lighting with accent colors (maybe brand color for ProofButton)
- **Animation Style**: Modern, clean, slightly 3D. Not overly gamified—professional and trustworthy.
- **VoiceOver** (Confident, clear narrator—different voice than emotional sections):
  - "Your press is the power source."
  - (pause for visual impact)
  - "Kinetic energy harvesting powers the button instantly."
  - "902 MHz wireless signal to your hub. No interference. No crowding."
  - "Hub sends text message via WiFi."
  - "Text message to any phone. No app needed."
  - "No batteries. Ever."

- **On-Screen Text** (animated, appearing with visual elements):
  - "PRESS" (above button)
  - "KINETIC ENERGY" (near capacitor)
  - "WIRELESS" (near signal)
  - "HUB" (near WiFi router)
  - "SMS" (near cloud)
  - "TEXT MESSAGE" (on phone screen)
  - Final screen: "No batteries. Works with any phone. Just your press."

- **Sound**:
  - Button click at start
  - Satisfying electronic "charging" sound as energy is captured
  - Subtle tech sounds (subtle beeps, wireless chimes) as signal travels
  - Final pleasant chime as action completes

- **Music**: Tech-forward, modern, uplifting. Not harsh or cold—friendly and accessible. Builds slightly from 45-55s.
- **Timing**: 45-55 seconds (10-second explainer)

**Key Notes**:
- This is the "wow factor" section
- Explain the technology without overwhelming
- Visually show that no batteries = no maintenance = better for environment
- Animation should feel premium and trustworthy
- End this section with the key differentiator: "No batteries. Ever."

---

### SECTION 5: CREDIBILITY (55-70 seconds)

**Tone**: Confident, humble, factual. Build trust. This is where the manufacturing background matters.

---

**Scene 5A: Jared Eggett Introduction | Interior, Modern Office or Manufacturing Space**

- **Visual**: Jared Eggett (founder) appears on camera in a clean, professional but approachable setting. Warm lighting. Perhaps in front of manufacturing equipment or family photos on wall—suggests both expertise and personal stake.
- **Camera**: Medium shot, eye-level, facing camera directly (intimate, trustworthy)
- **Lighting**: Warm, professional. Key light from front-left, subtle fill light to avoid harsh shadows
- **Action**: Jared speaks directly to camera, confident but warm. Genuine, not overly polished.
- **VoiceOver / Direct Quote** (Jared's voice):
  - "We didn't start in a garage."
  - (slight pause for emphasis)
  - "We started in a factory."

- **On-Screen Text**: "Jared Eggett | Founder, ProofButton" (appears as lower-third graphic)
- **Sound**: Jared's voice, natural room tone, subtle background ambience
- **Music**: Slight dip in volume; lets voice take focus. Underlying confidence builds.
- **Timing**: 55-60 seconds (5-second beat)

**Key Notes**:
- Direct camera address builds connection and trust
- "Didn't start in a garage" is a callback to the tech startup cliche and positions ProofButton as serious/legitimate
- "Started in a factory" establishes manufacturing credibility immediately

---

**Scene 5B: Manufacturing Credibility | B-Roll of Manufacturing/Factory**

- **Visual**: Quick B-roll montage (2-3 second cuts) of:

  - Skilled technicians working (hands-on craftsmanship)

  - Completed buttons being packaged
  - OR: Jared walking through manufacturing space, pointing out specific equipment/capabilities

- **Lighting**: Bright, clean factory lighting. Shows transparency.
- **Action**: Show the scale and sophistication of manufacturing. Even if it's modest, make it look competent and real.
- **VoiceOver** (Jared's voice, continuing):
  - "Twenty years of manufacturing expertise."

- **On-Screen Text** (optional, as lower-third info):
  - "20+ years manufacturing experience"

- **Sound**: Subtle factory ambience (machinery, precision sounds—not loud), Jared's voice over
- **Music**: Continues to build confidence; modern and capable-sounding
- **Timing**: 60-68 seconds (8-second montage)

**Key Notes**:
- Show real manufacturing (even if modest, be honest about it)
- "Tooling done. Production ready." is critical—addresses backer fears about delays
- "We built these for our own family first" is the ultimate credibility statement

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Overall Music & Sound Design

**0-5s**: Soft, comedic piano. Establish the problem with light humor.

**5-20s**: Tempo increases gradually. Comedic stings add to the montage chaos. Tension builds. Culminates at second 20 with a peak moment—we need a solution.

**20-45s**: Sharp tonal shift to uplifting, triumphant, family-oriented music. Each button press is rewarded with satisfying mechanical and electronic feedback. By second 45, we've "solved" the problems and the music swells. This is the emotional peak.

**45-55s**: Music dips slightly to let technical explanation shine, but remains confident and modern. Tech-forward underscore. "No batteries. Ever." landing on music sting.

**55-70s**: Builds credibility with confident, grounded underscore. Jared's voice takes focus. Warm, trustworthy tone in music.

**70-80s**: Energetic, motivational CTA music. Builds back to urgency and excitement.

**80-90s**: Grand, memorable finale. Tagline lands on a satisfying musical resolve. Audience should feel inspired and remember the brand.

### Voiceover Guidance

- **Hook (0-5s)**: Mom's natural, warm voice. Slightly exasperated—funny, relatable.
- **Problem Montage (5-20s)**: Mix of real family voices (parents yelling, kids responding). Natural, unscripted feel.
- **Solution (20-45s)**: Warm, confident narrator OR Jared's voice. Professional but approachable. Each action gets a simple confirmation (like the phone notifications).
- **How It Works (45-55s)**: Different voice—tech-confident, clear, authoritative. Not condescending. Make the tech exciting.
- **Credibility (55-70s)**: Jared's voice. Direct, honest, humble. This is the emotional truth of the founder.
- **CTA (70-80s)**: Motivational, clear, direct. Could be Jared or a professional voice.
- **Logo/Tagline (80-90s)**: Could be anyone—Jared, a child, a warm narrator. The tagline is the star here.

### Visual Style & Color Palette

- **Warm, naturalistic aesthetic**: Real family homes. Natural lighting (morning sun, afternoon light, evening glow). Not overly stylized.
- **Color Palette**: Warm neutrals (creams, warm grays) + accent colors:
  - Payson, UT home = warm earth tones, wood, soft furnishings
  - ProofButton brand color (TBD) should pop subtly in buttons and UI elements
  - Smart home devices (Alexa, Google Home) can be silver/white modern accents
- **Button Design**: Buttons should be visible, beautiful, and different (custom faces for medicine, chickens, toilet paper, dinner, goodnight). Each one should feel premium and designed.
- **Smart Home UI**: Clean, modern. Phone notifications, Alexa display, smart light transitions should look premium and seamless.

### Camera & Production Quality

- **Family Scenes (Sections 1-3, 5)**: High production value (4K if possible), but intentionally naturalistic. NOT overly stylized. Real homes, real lighting. Maybe handheld in places to feel authentic.
- **Animation (Section 4)**: Professional, clean, modern. Not cartoonish. Think Apple/Dyson quality.
- **B-Roll Manufacturing (Section 5)**: Documentary-style. Real factory, real process. Honest but flattering.
- **CTA & Logo (Sections 6-7)**: Clean, graphic design. Professional, memorable.

- [ ] Animation explains the technology without jargon
- [ ] Credibility section addresses founder expertise and real manufacturing
- [ ] CTA is crystal clear with price point and action
- [ ] Logo/Tagline is memorable and brandable
