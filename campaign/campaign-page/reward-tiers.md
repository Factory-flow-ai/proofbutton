# ProofButton Reward Tiers Documentation

Detailed reward tier structure with pricing rationale, COGS analysis, and tier descriptions for Kickstarter campaign.

---

## Reward Tier Structure Overview

ProofButton will offer 7 primary reward tiers plus add-ons, designed to capture different customer segments while maintaining healthy margins.

**Important Note:** All prices below are recommended placeholders based on typical hardware manufacturing + Kickstarter fees. Final pricing should be validated against:
- Actual COGS from Ebelong factory
- Real fulfillment costs (shipping, customs, handling)
- Kickstarter platform fees (8%)
- Desired profit margin (20-30%)

## Stretch Goal Pricing Impact

**Important:** Stretch goals don't change tier pricing. Instead:

1. **At each stretch goal:** Unlock new FREE features for ALL backers at their pledge level
2. **Examples:**
   - $50K: Everyone gets native Google Home integration (at fulfillment)
   - $75K: Everyone gets HomeKit/Matter support (at fulfillment)
   - $100K: Everyone gets extra custom face design (2 instead of 1)

This approach:
- ✓ Maintains original pledge value (no price confusion)
- ✓ Rewards ALL backers equally (fairness)
- ✓ Creates urgency to hit stretch goals (community benefit)
- ✓ Avoids pricing complexity

## Kickstarter Fee Impact (Important)

**Kickstarter takes 8%** of all pledges. This means:

- **$49 pledge → Kickstarter gets $3.92 → You receive $45.08**
- **$95 pledge → Kickstarter gets $7.60 → You receive $87.40**
- **$79 pledge → Kickstarter gets $6.32 → You receive $72.68**

**Prices listed above are BEFORE Kickstarter fees.** Your actual funding received is 92% of pledge price. Plan accordingly.

## Fulfillment Cost Breakdown (Example: Standard $49 Tier)

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| ProofButton unit manufacturing | $20 |
| Custom face printing | $2 |
| Hub (if applicable) | $16 |
| Packaging & insert design | $1.50 |
| Magnetic mounting hardware | $0.50 |
| USPS shipping (USA avg) | $5 |
| **Subtotal** | **$45** |
| Kickstarter fee (8%) | $3.92 |
| **Received after fees** | **$45.08** |
| **Profit after fulfillment** | **~$0.08** |

**Wait, that's too thin!**

This is why tiers are staggered. You expect:
- Some people buy Early Bird ($39) = thin margin
- Most people buy Standard ($49) = break-even to small margin
- Some people buy Premium ($79+) = healthier margin
- Add-ons = high margin (80%+ profit)
- Corporate tiers = volume makes up for lower per-unit margin

**Average margin across all pledges should be 20-25%.**

## Pricing Validation Checklist

**Before finalizing prices, validate:**

- [ ] Actual COGS from Ebelong factory (get quote for full production run)
- [ ] Real international shipping costs (get quotes from DHL, FedEx, USPS)
- [ ] Fulfillment warehouse costs (storage, packing, labeling)
- [ ] Custom face printing costs at scale (test with factory)
- [ ] Hub COGS (is it really $15-18?)
- [ ] Payment processing fees (Kickstarter already accounts for, but verify)
- [ ] Marketing/operational costs (how many backers needed to break even on campaign costs?)

**Formula for final pricing:**

```
Final Price = (COGS + Shipping + Fulfillment + Overhead) / (1 - Platform Fee - Profit Margin%)

Example:
(30 + 6 + 3 + 5) / (1 - 0.08 - 0.25) = $44 / 0.67 = $66

So for a $30 COGS product with $6 shipping and $3 fulfillment, with 8% Kickstarter fee and 25% target margin, you need ~$66 price point.
```

## Tier Strategy Recommendations

**1. Early Bird:** Make it compelling. $39 is $10 cheaper than standard. People who pay early get rewarded. This builds momentum and social proof.

**2. Standard:** This is your bread-and-butter. Price it where most people feel it's fair. $49 feels right for a quality wireless button.

**3. Premium Tiers:** $79+ tiers serve power users and smart home enthusiasts. They get hub + extras. Higher value perceived = higher margin acceptable.

**4. Add-Ons:** High margin items. Customers who already trust you (backed the campaign) are happy to add accessories. These improve your bottom line.

**5. Corporate Tier:** Opens new market (HR departments, companies) and increases average order value. Even with 25% discounts, volume makes it worthwhile.

## Pricing Psychology

**Why these specific prices?**

- **$39 Early Bird:** "I'm getting a deal" feeling (Flic is $25, but this has more value)
- **$49 Standard:** "Fair price for a quality product" (not cheap, not expensive, just right)
- **$95 Double:** "Better than two singles ($98)" - people like bundles
- **$79 All-In:** "Premium experience" (hub justifies higher price, adds value)
- **$189 Team:** "Works out to $47 per unit, so slight discount + premium service"

All prices are "charm pricing" (ending in 9) which psychologically feels cheaper than round numbers.

## Final Notes

- These are **recommendations** based on typical hardware Kickstarter campaigns
- **Validate all COGS with Ebelong before launching**
- Consider your gross margin target (I recommend 25-30% minimum)
- Price testing can happen via early feedback from Reddit/community
- If manufacturing costs are higher than estimates, raise all prices by 10-15%
- If you get cheaper than estimated, keep prices and pocket better margin (don't lower prices)

The goal: price where customers feel they're getting value, you're profitable, and you can deliver on promises.
