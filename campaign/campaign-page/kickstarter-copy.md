## How It Works

### **Three Steps to Accountability**

**Step 1: Label Your Button**
Pick what you need to track. "Medicine." "Chickens." "Dishes." "Practice Piano." "Gym." "Homework." "Bedtime." Download our custom label templates, print them, or engrave them. Peel off the adhesive backing. Stick the label on.

**Step 2: Stick It Anywhere**
Mount it wherever the action happens. Bathroom mirror for medicine. Chicken coop for feeding. Kitchen fridge for toilet paper reminders. Entryway for "shoes off." Use the peel-and-stick adhesive or optional screw mount. It's weatherproof, so outdoors works too.

**Step 3: Press It. Get a Text.**
When the task is done, press the button. That's it. One satisfying *click*. The button generates its own power from that press. The signal travels via Bluetooth to your ProofButton hub (placed anywhere WiFi reaches). The hub translates the signal and:

- **Sends a text message** to whoever you designated (mom, dad, caregiver, guardian—any phone number)
- **Logs a timestamp** (Proof, forever, visible in the web portal or app)
- **Optional smart home integration** (send notification to Alexa, Google Home, Home Assistant—but not required)
- **Works offline too** (hub stores timestamps locally if WiFi goes down, syncs when it's back)

**The hub does the heavy lifting.** One hub controls up to 32 buttons. That tiny white box does all the WiFi translation, the SMS sending, the logging. Plug it in once. Set it up via QR code on your phone (2 minutes to scan, enter phone numbers, done). Forget about it.

**No app needed. No subscription.** Your buttons work forever. Setup is pure simplicity: scan QR code, add phone numbers, press button. The hub stores the timestamps locally. When WiFi is up, texts send instantly. When WiFi is down, the hub logs it anyway.

**Why 902 MHz instead of Bluetooth?** Simple: reliability. The 2.4 GHz band where Bluetooth lives is packed with traffic—your phone, headphones, smartwatch, WiFi router, your neighbor's devices—all competing for the same airspace. 902 MHz is wide open. It's a dedicated, uncrowded frequency designed for reliability. When your kid presses the medicine button at 7:30 AM, that signal needs to reach the hub. Every time. No exceptions. 902 MHz has been proven in commercial building automation for 20+ years. Millions of devices deployed. Lower frequency means better range through walls, better penetration, less interference. Your proof gets through.
