After talking to hundreds of families, we've noticed something: once people get their first ProofButton, they immediately want more.

Here are 10 different ways families are planning to use ProofButton:

---

**1. Medicine Accountability**
Your kid needs to take their daily medication. They press the button. You get a text message with a timestamp. If they miss the deadline (7:30 AM on school days, 9 AM on weekends), you get a reminder text. The argument is over. The text is the proof.

**2. Chicken Coop Check**
Your farm kids are supposed to feed and water the chickens every morning. One button by the coop. One press = they get a text confirmation. You get a text notification. No more "I forgot" or "I thought you did it." The chickens are fed. You got proof. Proven.

**3. Toilet Paper Alert (Bathroom 1)**
One button per bathroom. When the roll runs out, press it. You get a text. Someone actually restocks it. Revolutionary.

**4. Toilet Paper Alert (Bathroom 2)**
Yes, a whole separate button. Because three kids, one roll per bathroom, and apparently everyone waits until it's too late. You get a text from each bathroom. Problem solved.

**5. Toilet Paper Alert (Bathroom 3)**
See above. For the master bath. You're welcome.

**6. Dinner is Ready**
Your teenager is in their room. Your spouse is upstairs. Your youngest is buried in a video game. You press this button. They all get a text: "Dinner is ready." No WiFi required. Works when internet is down. No yelling. No repeating yourself. Just a button press and texts arrive.

**7. Wake Up Reminder**
Your kid is supposed to be up and moving by 7 AM. They press this button when they're actually awake. You get a text proof they're moving. Great for parents of teens who operate on a different timezone.

**8. Goodnight Ritual**
Before bed, your child presses the goodnight button. You get a text confirming they went to sleep. It becomes a ritual. A habit. A win. Kids love pressing the button. Parents love the confirmation text.

**9. Help/Panic Button**
Stick one in the garage. One in the backyard. One in your elderly parent's room. When someone needs help — emergency or just questions — they press the button. You get a text instantly. No need to hunt for someone. Works without WiFi.

**10. Custom Button (What will you use it for?)**
We have families thinking about buttons for:
- "Homework is done"
- "Trash needs to go out"
- "I cleaned my room"
- "Dishes are finished"
- "I got home safe from work/school"
- "I locked the front door"
- "I'm leaving for soccer practice"
- "Mom, I need you" (for younger kids)

The pattern is always the same: **a conversation that used to require nagging, arguing, or verification. Now it's just a button press and a notification.**
