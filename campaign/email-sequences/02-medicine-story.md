## Email Body

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

I want to tell you the story behind ProofButton. Not the polished version. The real one.

My son Linkin has Type 1 diabetes. Every single day, he needs to take his medicine. Every. Single. Day. No exceptions. No days off.

For years, mornings in our house looked like this:

"Linkin, did you take your medicine?"

"Yes, Dad."

"Are you sure?"

"Yes, Dad."

Five minutes later, I'd find his medicine bottle untouched on the counter. He didn't forget maliciously. He just... forgot. He's a kid. Kids forget. That's what kids do.

So we'd argue. I'd be frustrated. He'd be upset. His mom would jump in. We'd all be wound up before 7:30 AM. And I still didn't know if he'd taken his medicine or not.

This happened hundreds of times. Maybe thousands.

It was exhausting. It was stressful. And it was putting a strain on our relationship. I didn't want to be the "did you take your medicine" dad. But diabetes doesn't care about what I want.

**Then I thought: what if the button was the proof?**

What if instead of asking, I just gave Linkin a button and told him: "When you take your medicine, press this button so I know it's done."

So we built it. A button he could press. A notification I could see. A conversation that could finally be over.

The first morning, I was skeptical. But Linkin pressed it at 7:18 AM. I got a text message with a timestamp. Literally a text. On my phone. My wife got one too. Both of us knew, instantly, that he'd taken his medicine.

No app needed. No WiFi. Just a text message arriving on any phone that texts.

No more "Did you take your medicine?"

No more arguments before school.

No more guessing.

The button pressed. The text arrived. And that text—that simple proof—it changed everything.

**That's why we built ProofButton.**

Not because we invented some fancy technology (though it is pretty cool — it powers itself, harvests energy from your fingers, and needs no batteries ever).

We built it because accountability shouldn't require argument. Proof shouldn't require trust in memory. And parents shouldn't have to be the accountability police.

We built it because mornings should be better.

Over the past few years, we've talked to hundreds of families. And every single one of them has their version of Linkin's story:

- The parent checking if chores are done
- The family trying to remind everyone about dinner
- The elderly parent's kids wanting to know they pressed the help button
- The teenager who needs accountability tools, not confrontation

Every one of them had a conversation they were tired of having.

Every one of them needed proof that didn't require an argument.

**ProofButton is for them. And for us. And if you have a conversation like this in your house, it might be for you too.**

We're launching on Kickstarter soon. And we're asking you to be part of the solution.

Because accountability shouldn't be hard. It should be a button press.

Jared Eggett
Founder, ProofButton

P.S. — Linkin still takes his medicine every day. And he still presses his button every morning. Some habits, once they're solid, just stick around. That's exactly what we're hoping for.
